How much of your city is actually under trees?

Leaves.PH is now a map you can explore, not a report you read. Open it, drag the
year from 2019 to 2026, and watch Metro Manila's tree cover shift. Search your
barangay. Click any detected tree and see it from the sky.

What it shows: about a tenth of the National Capital Region sits under canopy,
and most of that is in one city. Quezon City, held up by the La Mesa watershed,
UP Diliman, and the Wack Wack greens, carries the regional average on its own.
Take QC out and the figure roughly halves.

Under the hood: 242,810 individual tree crowns mapped from Sentinel-2 imagery and
Meta's 1-metre canopy-height data, classified by a model trained on 656
hand-checked high-resolution points. Open data, open code, reproducible from a
clean clone.

It is a measurement from public satellite data, not an accusation about any
specific site. Find your barangay and have a look.

Explore: https://leaves.ph
